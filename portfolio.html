I'm Elvis.

🧑🏾

I'm a FullStack Developer working remotely from Rivers, Nigeria.

Over the past few years, I've pushed into different parts of web creation, from crafting UI layouts to coding interactive features. I'm proud of how far I've evolved.

These days, I focus on personal projects, tutorials or school.

Let's collaborate if you're committed to sustainability, education, equality, or carbon neutrality.

I'm committed to leaving the world better for those who come after us, and I aim to contribute however I can. If you value that too, I'd love to talk.

01
Make it
I sketch wireframes and make prototypes. Talking through tactile designs existing in the browser is worth its weight. Design tools only carry you so far; the rest should be realized with a link my team can rally around.
02
Collaborate
Good design is not created in a vacuum but rather in a shared space. It must be facilitated and iterated upon as a team. I aim to include stakeholders in my design process and create a collaborative environment that welcomes and encourages feedback.
03
Build Performant Code
Performance is not a feature; it's a requirement. I focus on optimizing asset delivery, minimizing render-blocking resources, and writing efficient JavaScript to ensure fast loading times and a smooth 60fps interaction experience.
04
Maintain Quality & Scale
I prioritize code health through strict component architecture, proper typing (e.g., TypeScript), and comprehensive unit testing. This ensures the codebase remains maintainable, scalable, and resilient as the product grows.

Before I stepped fully into web development, I worked as a virtual assistant. A lot of my early tasks involved handling emails, content, and small technical fixes — long before I knew I'd fall in love with writing real code. Those moments were my first glimpse into how the web actually works.

As I grew more curious, I began studying web development and building projects of my own. From recreating classic games like the Simon Game to experimenting with UI layouts and Tailwind, every project has pushed me to understand not just how things look, but how they function behind the scenes. Now, my learning journey is driven by the excitement of exploring new languages, new tools, and new challenges.

I believe in building in public, documenting my process, and sharing what I learn — even the messy parts. That's how I grow, and hopefully how I can help others grow too.
